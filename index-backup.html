Road funding basics

GAS TAX:

Our current funding source for roads and bridges

The construction and maintenance of highways is currently funded through a state gas tax of 49.4 cents per gallon that drivers pay at the pump.

THE FUNDING SOURCE WILL RUN OUT

Washington State law has set forth the goal of no more gas-powered cars by 2030. As more of the cars on our roads are electric or more fuel-efficient, funding from the gas tax is expected to decline.

In order to generate current levels of revenue, the gas tax would need to increase 1.7 cents per gallon per year. This would mean a gas tax of over 90 cents per gallon by 2040.

Why RUC?

A RUC IS:

  • Not an additional tax – drivers will not be double-charged
  • A sustainable funding source to replace the state gas tax
  • A per-mile charge based on the number of miles driven (not gallons of gas purchased)
  • More fair for low-income drivers and drivers of less fuel-efficient cars
  • A more equitable way to pay for roads – everyone pays for what they use regardless of what they drive
